## Price Elasticity of Demand and Price Elasticity of Supply

- Price elasticity
	- Ratio between the percentage change in the quantity demanded or supplied and the corresponding percent change in price
- Price elasticity of demand
	- Percentage change in the quantity demanded of a good or service divided by the percentage change in the price
- Price elasticity of supply
	- Percentage change in the quantity supplied divided by the percentage change in the price

- Elasticities
	- Elastic
	- Inelastic
	- Unitary
- Price elasticity of demand is always a negative number
	- Price inversely related to quantity
- Price elasticity of demand is reported as its absolute value
- Elastic demand/supply
	- The elasticity is greater than one
		- Indicating a high responsiveness to changes in price
- Inelastic demand/supply
	- Elasticity less than one
		- Low responsiveness to price changes
- Unitary elasticitit
	- Indicate proportional responsiveness of either demand or supply

## Polar Cases of Elasticity and Constant Elasticity

- Two extreme cases of elasticity
	- Elasticity equals zero
	- Elasticity is infinite
		- Constant unitary elasticity
- Infinite elasticity/perfect elasticity
	- Either the quantity demanded or supplied changes by an infinite amount in response to any change in the price at all
	- The supply and demand curve are horizontal
	- Goods with readily available inputs, and whose production can easily expand
	- Pizza, bread

- Zero elasticity/perfect inelasticity
	- A percentage change in price, no matter how large, results in zero change in quantity
	- Goods with limited supply of inputs
	- Diamond rings or housing prime locations

	- Either a supply or demand curve
	- A price change of one percent results in a quantity change of one percent

### Cross-Price Elasticity of Demand

- A change in the price of one good can shift the quantity demanded for another good
- Complemented goods
	- Drop in the price one good will lead to an increase in the other
		- Eggs and toast
- Substitues
	- A drop in the price of one good, will cause people to substitute toward that good, and reduce consumption of the other
		- Trains and plane tickets

- Cross price elasticity of demand
	- The price of one good is affecting the quantity demanded of a different good
	- Percentage change in the quantity of good A that is demanded as a result og a percentage change in the price of good B
