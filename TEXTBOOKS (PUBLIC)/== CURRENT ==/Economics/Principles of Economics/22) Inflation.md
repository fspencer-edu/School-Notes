## Tracking Inflation

- The power of inflation affect the goods and services, as well as the wages and income levels
- The inflation rate is the percentage change in the price levels from a combined price of a variety of goods and services

### The Price of a Basket of Goods

- Basket of goods and services
	- Consists of different items individuals, businesses, or organization buy
	- Changes in the prices of goods for which people spend a larger share of their incomes will matter more than items what people spend less one

### Index Numbers

- Index number
	- Reported price level in each period
	- Prices indices are used to calculate the average change in relative prices over time
- Base year
	- Starting point from which we measure changes in prices
	- An index number equal to 100

## How to Measure Changes in the Cost of Living

- Consumer Price Index (CPI)
	- Calculate the CPI based on the prices in a fixed basket of goods and services that are present the purchases of the average family of four
	- The change in the total cost of buying a fixed basket of goods, is not the same as the change in the cost of living
- Substitution bias
	- The rise in the price of fixed basket of good over time, tends to overstate the rise in a consumer's true cost of living
	- A consumer can substitute away from goods that have risen
- Quality/new goods bias
	- The rise in the price of a fixed basket of goods over time tends to overstate the rise in a consumer's true cost of living
	- Does not take into account how improvements in the quality of existing goods or invention of new goods improve the standard of living

- US Bureau of Labour Statistics (BLS)
	- Calculates CPI
	- 8 major categories
		- Food and beverages
		- Housing
		- Aperal
		- Transportation
		- Medical care
		- Recreation
		- Education and communication
		- Other goods and services

### The CPI and Core Inflation Index

- Core inflation index
	- Taking the CPI and excluding volatile economic variables
	- Energy and food prices
	- Gas prices

### Practical Solutions for the Substitution and the Quality/New Goods Biases

### Additional Price Indices - PPI, GDP Deflator, and More

- Producer Price Index (PPI)
	- Based on prices paid for supplies and inputs by producers of goods and services
	- Broken down into price indices for different industries, commodities, and stages of processing
- International price Index
	- Based on the prices of merchandise that is exported or imported
- Employment Cost Index
	- Measures wage inflation in the labour market
- GDP deflator
	- A price index that includes all the GDP components

## The Confusion Over Inflation

### The Land of Funny Money

- Over most periods, the inflation level in prices is roughly similar to the inflation level in wages
- If all prices, wages, and interest rates adjusted with inflation, then no one's purchasing power, profits, or payments would change
- Uneven inflation
	- Redistribution of purchasing power
	- Blurred price signals
	- Difficulties in long-term planning

### Unintended Redistributions of Purchasing Power

- US income tax is charged on the nominal interest received in dollar terms, without an adjustment for inflation
- Increases in wages may lag behind inflations for a year or two
- Extent to which wages keep up with inflation creates insecurity for workers
- Most pensions have been set as a fixed nominal dollar amount at retirement
- Defined contribution (DC) plans
	- Retirement savings plan where employees and employers contribute a fixed percentage or amount of salary into an individual account
	- Tax-deferred, and adjusted for inflation

- A borrower paying a fixed interest rate, benefits from inflation
	- Whereas the investor suffers
- When interest rates are fixed, rises in the rate of inflation penalizes suppliers of financial capital

### Blurred Price Signals

- Inflation means that we perceive price signals more vaguely
- High and variable inflation means that the incentives in the economy to adjust in response to changes in prices are weaker
- Markets will adjust toward their equilibrium prices and quantities more erratically and slowly
- Individuals will experience greater changes of surpluses and shortages

### Problems of Long-Term Planning

- Inflation can make long-term planning difficult
- Problem for businesses
	- Investing
	- Paying bills and wages
	- Collecting revenue
	- Suplus or shortage
- Tradeoff strikes
	- Less time is spend on improving products and services

### Any Benefits of Inflation?

- Impact of inflation will differ according to is rate
- Low inflation is also better than deflation which occurs with severe recessions
- Moderate inflation may help the economy by making wages in labour markets more flexible

## Indexing and Its Limitations

- Indexed
	- When a price, wage, or interest rate is adjusted automatically with inflation

### Indexing in Private Markets

- Cost-of-living adjustments (COLASs)
	- Guaranteed that their wages would keep up with inflation
	- COLA plus 3%
- Loans have built-in inflation adjustments
- Adjustable-rate mortgage (ARM)
	- Type of loan that one can use to purchase a home in which the interest rate varies with the rate if inflation
### Indexing in Government Programs

- US income tax code is designed so that as a person's income rises above certain levels, the tax rate on the marginal income earned rises as well
- Social Security programs provides two examples of indexing
	- Level of social security benefits increases each year with the CPI
	- Social security is funded by payroll taxes, which the government imposes on the income earned up to a certain amount
- Indexed bonds
### Might Indexing reduce Concern over Inflation?

- Indexing is always partial
- As partial inflation indexing spreads, the political opposition to inflation may diminish
- When more people are indexed against inflation and some are not, financially savvy businesses and investors may seek out ways to be protected, while others lose more
