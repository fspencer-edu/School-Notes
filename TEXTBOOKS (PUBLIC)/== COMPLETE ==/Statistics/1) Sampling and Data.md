# Definition of Statistics, Probability, and Key Terms

- Descriptive statistics
	- Organizing and summarizing data
- Inferential statistics
	- Use of probability to determine how confident that the conclusions are correct

## Probability

- Probability
	- Mathematical tool used to study randomness

- Population
	- A collections of persons, things, or objects under study
- Sample
	- A subset of the population intended to represent the population
- Sampling
	- Select a portion of the larger population and study that portion to gain information about the population
- Statistic
	- A number that represents a property of the sample
- Parameter
	- A numerical characteristic of the whole population that can be estimated by a statistic
- Representative sample
	- Samples that contain the characteristics of the population
- Variable
	- A characteristic or measurement that can be determined for each member of a population
	- Numerical or categorical
- Numerical variables
	- Values with equal units
- Categorical variables
	- A date type representing qualitative characteristics or groups
- Data
	- Actual values of the variable
- Datum
	- A single value
- Mean
	- Average of a set of numbers
- Proportion
	- Two ratios or fractions are equal, representing the same relative size of relationship

# Data, Sampling, and Variation in Data and Sampling

- Qualitative data
	- Result of categorizing ore describing attributes of a population
	- Also called categorical data
- Quantitative data
	- Results of counting or measuring attributes of a population
	- Discrete
	- Continuous
- Quantitative discrete data
	- Data takes on only certain numerical values
- Quantitative continuous data
	- Not made up of only counting numbers
	- Also includes fractions, decimals, or irrational numbers


## Qualitative Data Discussion

- Pie chart
	- Categories of data re represented by wedges in a circle and are proportional in size to the percent of individuals in each category
- Bar chart
	- Length of the bar for each category is proportional to the number or percent of individuals in each category
- Pareto chart
	- Consists of bars that are sorted into order by category size

### Percentages that Add to More/Less than 100%

- Individuals can be in more than one category

## Sampling

- A sample should have the same characteristics as the population it is representing
- Random sampling
	- Each member of a population initially has an equal change of being selected for the sample
- Simple random sample
	- Any group of n individuals is equally likely to be chosen as any other group of n individuals
- Other sampling methods
	- Stratified sample
	- Cluster sample
	- Systematic sample

**Stratified Sample**
- The population is divided into groups called strata and then take a proportionate number from each stratum

**Cluster Sample**
- Population id divided into groups and then randomly selected from each cluster

**Systematic Sample**
- Randomly select a starting point to take every nth piece of data from a listing of the population

**Convenience Sample**
- Using results that are readily available

- Sampling with replacement
	- Truly random sampling
	- Member that is picked goes back into the population
- Simple random sampling without replacement
	- A member is chosen only one
- Sampling errors
	- Small population size
- Non-sampling errors
	- Defective counting device
- Sampling bias
	- A sample is collected from a population and some members of that population are not as likely to be chosen as others

### Critical Evaluation

- A sample must be representative of the population
- Self-selected samples are often unreliable
- Samples that are too small are unreliable
- Undue influence
- Non-response or refusal or participation
- Causality
- Self-funded or self-interest studies
- Misleading use
- Confounding

## Variation in Data

- Variation
	- Present in any set of data
	- Change, difference, or deviation in the quality, amount, or level of something
	- Represents a modification from a standard of norm

# Frequency, Frequency Tables, and Levels of Measurement

## Rounding

- Round off only the final answer
- Carry intermediate results to at least twice as many decimal places as the final answer

## Levels of Measurement

- Level of measurements
	- The way a set of data is measured

- Nominal scale level
- Ordinal scale level
- Interval scale level
- Ratio scale level

## Frequency

- Frequency
	- The number of times a value of data occurs
- Relative frequency
	- The ratio of the number of times a value of the data occurs in the set of all outcomes to the total number of outcomes
- Cumulative relative frequency
	- Accumulation of the previous relative frequencies

# Experimental Design and Ethics

- Explanatory variable
	- One variable that causes change in another
- Response variable
	- The affect variable
- Treatments
	- Different values of the explanatory variable
- Experimental unit
	- A single object or individual to be measured
- Lurking variables
	- Additional variables that can cloud a study
- Random assignment
	- An experimental technique used to place participants into treatment or control groups using randomization, ensuring each participant has an equal change of being in any group

- Control group
	- Researchers set assign one treatment group with no planned treatments
- Placebo
	- A treatment that cannot influence the response variable
- Blinding
	- Preserves the power of suggestion in a randomized experiment
- Double-blind experiment
	- Both the subjects and the researchers involved with the subjects are blind

## Ethics

- Institutional Review Boards (IRB)
	- Committees
	- Risks to participants must be minimized and reasonable with respect to projected benefits
	- Participants must give informed consent
	- Data collected from individuals must be protected for their privacy
