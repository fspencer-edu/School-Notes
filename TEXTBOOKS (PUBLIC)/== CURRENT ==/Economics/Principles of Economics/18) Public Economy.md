## Voter Participation and Costs of Elections

- Rational ignorance
	- People will not vote if the costs of becoming informed and voting are too high
- People who are more settled or more connected to society tend to vote more frequently

## Special Interest Politics

- Special interest groups
	- Groups that are small in number relative to the nation, but are well organized and focused on a specific issue
	- Pressure legislators to enact public policies that do not benefit society as a whole

### Identifiable Winners, Anonymous Losers
- Policies that impose price control may look as if they benefit renters and imposed cost only on landlords
	- Rent control
- Although landlords may reduce the number of rental units available in the area
- Policies that block imports will benefit the firms that compete with those imports
	- Consumers will have to pay some of these increased costs
- Tax breaks and spending programs

### Pork Barrels and Logrolling

- Politicians have an incentive to ensure they they spending government money in their home state or district
- Pork-barrel spending
	- Legislation that benefits mainly a single political district
- Watchdog organization
	- A non profit that attempt to quantify the amount of pork barrel spending
- Logrolling
	- An action in which all members of a group of legislators agree to vote for a package of otherwise unrelated laws that they individually favour, can encourage pork barrel spending

## Flaws in the Democratic System of Government

- Most developed countries have a democratic system of government
	- Citizens express their opinions through votes and those votes affect the direction of the country
- Median voter theory
	- Politicians will try to match policies to what pleases the median voter preferences
- Voting cycle
	- How government decisions and policies change over time around elections
	- Policy decisions -> Influenced by winning votes -> Influenced by election timing

### Where Is Government's Self-Correcting Mechanism

- Governments do not sell their products in a market
	- Receive tax dollars
	- Not challenged by competitors
	- The pressure government faces to changes it bureaucracy, to seek greater efficiency

### A Balanced View of Markets and Government

- Markets are useful and flexible through which society can allocate its scarce resources
- Markets may sometimes produce unwanted results
	- Monopoly
	- Pollution
	- Poverty
	- Inequality of incomes
	- Discrimination
	- Failure to provide insurance
- Government may play a role in addressing problems of markets
