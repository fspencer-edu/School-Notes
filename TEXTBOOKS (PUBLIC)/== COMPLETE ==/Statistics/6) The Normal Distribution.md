
- Normal distribution

$X \sim N(\micro, \sigma)$

$\micro$ = mean
$\sigma$ = standard deviation

**Probability Density Function for Normal Distribution**

$f(x) = \frac{1}{\sigma \sqrt{2\pi}}\cdot e^{-\frac{1}{2}\cdot(\frac{x-\micro}{\sigma})^2}$

- Curve is symmetric about a vertical line through the mean, $\micro$
- The mean is the same as the median, because the graph is symmetric
- The normal distribution depends only on the mean and standard deviation
- Area under the curve is 1, and changes in the standard deviation causes a change in the shape of the curve
	- $\sigma$ => Compressed or stretched
	- $\micro$ => Left or right shift

# The Standard Normal Distribution

- The standard normal distribution is a normal distribution of standardized values called z-scores
- A z-score is measured in units of the standard deviation
- The mean for the standard normal distribution is zero, and the standard deviation is one

## Z-Scores
- If X is a normally distributed random variable
- Z-score represents how many standard deviations the value x is above (right) or below (left) the mean

$Z \sim N(\micro, \sigma)$

$z = \frac{x - \micro}{\sigma}$

**Example**
$N \sim N(5, 6)$

$z = \frac{x - \micro}{\sigma} = \frac{17-5}{6}=2$

$\therefore x=17$ is two standard deviations ($2\sigma$) above the mean $\micro = 5$
