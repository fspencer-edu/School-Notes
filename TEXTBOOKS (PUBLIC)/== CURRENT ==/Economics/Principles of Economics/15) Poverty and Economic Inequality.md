## The Poverty Trap

- Poverty trap
	- A self-reinforcing, cyclical mechanism where poverty causes conditions that make is difficult to escape
	- Passes from generation to generation
	- As a person starts to earn money, they lose government support, and results in no net gain for working

## The Safety Net

- Safety net
	- Programs that offer some protection for those who find themselves without jobs or income

### Temporary Assistance for Needy Families

- Dependent Children (AFDC)
	- Provides cash payments to mothers with children who were below the poverty line
- Temporary Assistance for Needy Families (TANF)

- TANF
	- Federal government gives a fixed amount of money to each state
	- Each state uses the money for almost any program with an antipoverty component
		- Low income housing
		- Reduce teenage pregnancy
		- Public schools
	- For state to keep TANF grands, they must impose work requirements so that most of those receiving TANF benefits are working (or attending school)
	- No one can receive TANF benefits with federal money for more than a total of 5 years over their lifetime
	- Requires that welfare recipients work, and length they receive benefits for
- Department of Health and Human Services
	- Reports cost of living

## Government Polices to Reduce Income Inequality

- Most workers receive relatively low earning in their first few jobs, and increase as they age
- People's preferences and desires differ
- Income
	- Flow of money received
	- Measure monthly or annually
- Wealth
	- Sum of the value of assets
	- Money, financial investments, pension fund, and value of home
- In the US, wealth distribution is more unequal than income distribution
	- Differences in income can accumulate over time to make large differences in wealth

- 3 main tools to reduce economic inequality
	- Redistribution from high incomes to low incomes
	- Ladder of opportunity is widely available
	- Tax on inheritance

### Redistribution

- Redistribution
	- Taking income from those with higher incomes and providing income to those with lower incomes
- Progressive tax system
	- Federal income tax
	- The rich pay a higher percent in income taxes than the poor
- Effective income tax
	- Total taxes paid divided by total income

### The Ladder of Opportunity

- Improved day care
- Enrichment programs for preschoolers
- Improved public schools
- Internships and apprenticeships
- Loads and grants for students
- Public support for universities or colleges
- Retraining and acquiring new skills
- Prohibiting discrimination in job markets and housing on the basis of race, gender, age, and disability
### Inheritance Taxes

- Estate tax
	- A tax imposed on the value of an inheritance
	- Limit how much wealth one can pass on as an inheritance
