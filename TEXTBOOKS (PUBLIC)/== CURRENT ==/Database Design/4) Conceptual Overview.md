## Completing the Design Process

- Implement and follow the database design process from beginning to end

## Missing Statement and Objectives

- Missing statements
	- Establishes the purpose of the database
	- Provides a distinct focus and purpose
	- Focused on database developer, database, and management personnel
- Mission objectives
	- Statements that represent the general tasks users can perform
	- Focused on end users

## Analyzing the Current Database

- Legacy database
	- Already in existence and in use
- Paper-based database
	- A loose collection of forms, folders
- Analysis the way a business collects and presents the data
- How the organization uses the data
- Conducting interviews with users and management to identify how they interact with the database on a daily basis
- Compile an initial list of fields
	- Refined list constitutes organization's fundamental data requirements and provides a starting point for the design of a new database
- Send a field list to users and management for further refinement and feedback

## Creating the Data Structures

 - Tables are the first structures defined in the database
	 - Determine
		 - Subjects that the table will represent from the mission objectives
		 - Establish associated fields
- Establish field specifications for each field
	- Conduct interviews with users and management to identify specific field characteristics

## Table Relationships

- Establish table relationships
- Identify
	- Relationships
	- Relationship characteristics
	- Relationship level integrity
- Establish a logical connection between tables
	- Add primary and foreign keys, with a linking table
- Determine type of participation, and degree of participation

## Business Rules

- Conduct interviews, identify limitations on various aspects of the database, establish business, rules, and define and implement validation tables
- Limitations
	- Specific
	- General
- Establishing business rules is an ongoing, iterative process

## Defining Views

- Determine and define views
- Determine respective data, and user levels

## Data Integrity

- Review the final database structure for data integrity
- Review each table to ensure that it meets the criteria of a property design ed table
- Resolve inconsistencies or problems
- Review and check field specifications
- Review validity of each relationship, confirm relationship type, and participation
- Review business rules
