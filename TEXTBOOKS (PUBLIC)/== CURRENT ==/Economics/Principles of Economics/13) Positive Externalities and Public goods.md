	- Railroads
	- Mass production
- Economic meltdowns during the "installment" phase of a widespread new technology

## Public Goods

- Public good
	- Products or services that are both non-excludable and non-rivalrous
- National defense

### The Definition of a Public Good

- Non-excludable
	- It is costly or impossible to exclude someone from using the good
- Non-rival
	- When on person uses the public good, another can also use it

- Public good and positive externalities
	- Police protection
	- Public health funding

- Public good and negative externalities
	- Investments in education
	- Investments in private companies
	- Patents

### The Free Rider Problem of Public Goods

- Free rider
	- People have an incentive to let others pay for the public good and then to "free ride" on the purchases of other
	- Tax evaders
	- Vaccines
	- Public transport fare evasion
	- Neighbour's wifi

### Common Resources and the "Tragedy of the Commons"

- Non-excludable and rivalrous
	- Called common resources
		- Conches in the Caribbean
- Tragedy of the commons
	- Problem of property rights
	- No individual has the incentive to protect or property harvest
	- Requires harvest limits, shorter fishing seasons, and licenses
- Catch shares
	- Regulators establish a total allowable catch, and then fishermen are allocated a portion of that total allowable catch
- Non-tragedy of the commons
	- People would only avoid depletion of common resources if they were forced to by regulatory laws and property rights
	- Those who benefits the most from a resource are in close proximity to it (farmers)
	- Resource is better managed without external influences
