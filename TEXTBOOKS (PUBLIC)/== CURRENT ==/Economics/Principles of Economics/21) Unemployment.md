## How Economists Define and Compute Unemployment Rate

### Who's In or Out of the Labour Force?

- Adult population
	- Employed
	- Unemployed
	- People with out a job for other reasons (out of the labour force)
		- Retired
		- Looking after children
		- Voluntary break

- Employed
	- Currently working for pay
- Unemployed
	- Out of work and actively looking for a job
- Out of the labour force
	- Out of paid work and not actively looking for a job
- Labour force
	- The number of employed plus the unemployed

### Calculating the Unemployment Rate

- Unemployment rate
	- Percentage of adults who are in the labour force, but do not have jobs

### Hidden Unemployment

- Mislabeled workers
	- Part time or temporary jobs
	- Contract

- Underemployment
	- Those who are trained or skilled for one type of level of work but are working in a lower paying jobs that does not utilize their skills
- Discouraged workers
	- Those who have stopped looking for employment, and no longer counted in the unemployed

### Labour Force Participation Rate

- Labour force participation rate
	- Percentage of adults in an economy who are either employed or who are unemployed and looking for a job

### The Establishment Payroll Survey

- Bureau of Labour Statistics (BLS)
	- Reports on the number of jobs created from establishment payroll survey
	- Does not count the self-employed

### How Does the US Bureau of Labour Statistics Collect the US Unemployment Data

- Current Population Survey (CPS)
	- Measure the percentage of the labour force that is unemployed
- Bureau of Labour Statistics' establishment payroll surve (EPS)
	- Payroll survey that measure the new change in jobs created for the month

## What Causes Changes in Unemployment over the Short Run

### Cyclical Unemployment

- Cyclical unemployment (business cycle)
	- Variation in unemployment that the economy causes moving from expansion to recession or vice versa

<img src="/images/Pasted image 20260405203529.png" alt="image" width="500">

- Current Population Survey
	- Counts workers as unemployed when looking for a higher wage

### Why Wages Might Be Sticky Downward

- An economic concept where nominal wages are resistant to decreasing, even during economic downturns, high unemployment or reduced demand of labour
- Implicit contract
	- The employer will try to keep wages from falling when the economy is eak or the business is having trouble
	- Employee will not expect huge salary increases then the economy increases

- Efficiency wage theory
	- Worker's productivity depends on their pay
- Adverse selection of wage cuts argument
	- If an employer reacts to poor business conditions by reducing wages for all workers, then the best workers, will leave
- Insider-outsider model
	- Those already working for firms are "insiders" and new employees are "outsiders"
- Relative wage coordination argument
	- Even if most workers are willing to see a decline in their own wages in bad economic times, there is no way for a decentralized economy to implement such a plan

## What Causes Changes in Unemployment over the Long Run

- Cyclical unemployment explains way unemployment rises during a recession and falls during an economic expansion
- Natural rate of unemployment
	- The remaining level of unemployment that occurs even when the economy is healthy

### The Long Run - The Natural Rate of Unemployment

### Frictional Unemployment

- Frictional unemployment
	- Unemployment that occurs while workers move between jobs
	- May depend on the age distribution of the population
	- Unemployment rates are lower for people between 25-54, compared to 55 and over

### Structural Unemployment

- Structural unemployment
	- Individuals who have no jobs because they lack skills valued by the labour market

### Natural Unemployment and Potential Real GDP

- National unemployment rate
	- Full employment
	- Potential real GDP
- Economy is at full employment when the actual unemployment rate is equal to the national unemployment rate
- When the economy is at full employment, real GDP is equal to the potential real GDP
	- When below full employment, the unemployment rate is greater than the natural unemployment rate and real GDP is less than potential
- When economy is above, the unemployment rate is less than the natural, and the real GDP is greater than the potential
- Operating above potential is only possible for a short period of time

### Productivity Shifts and the Natural Rate of Unemployment

- Worker's productivity determines the level of wages in an economy

### Public Policy and the Natural rate of Unemployment

- Public policies asset the unemployed to find work
	- Unemployment insurance
	- Welfare
	- Food stamps
	- Government medical benefits
- Government regulations can make it harder to start a business by requiring that a new business obtain many permits and pay fees
	- Zoning laws
	- Operational hours

### The Natural Rate of Unemployment in Recent Years

- Reasons that employment will change
	- Social networking sites
	- Growth of the temporary worker industry
	- Aging generation
### The Natural Rate of Unemployment in Europe

- In high income economies, the natural rate of unemployment is relatively low
- Laws and regulations have lead to a high natural rate of unemployment that is much more prevalent in Europe than in the US
