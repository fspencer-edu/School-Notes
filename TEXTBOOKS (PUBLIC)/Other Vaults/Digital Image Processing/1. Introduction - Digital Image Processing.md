# What Is Digital Image Processing?

- An image may be defined as a 2D function, f(x, y)
- x and y are spatial coordinates
- The amplitude of f at any pair of coordinates is called the intensity of gray level of the image at that point
- When x, y, and f are finite, discrete quantities, the image is a digital image
- The field of digital image processing refers to processing digital images by a digital computer
- Elements are referred to as picture elements, image elements, pels, and pixels
- Pixel is the term more used to denote the elements of a digital image
- Imaging machines cover almost the entire EM spectrum, ranging from gamma to radio waves
	- Humans can only see visual band
- Image processing is when both the input and output of a process are images

- Low-level processes involved primitive operations
	- Image pre-processing to reduce noise
	- Constrast enhancement
	- Image sharpening
- Mid-level processes on images involve segmentation
	- Description of those objects
	- Classification
	- Outputs are extracts from images
- Higher-level processing involved image analysis
	- Cognitive functions
