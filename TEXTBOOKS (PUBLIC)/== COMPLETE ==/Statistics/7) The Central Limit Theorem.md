# The Central Limit Theorem for Sample Means (Averages)

- X is a random variable with a any distribution

$\micro_x$ = mean of x
$\sigma_X$ = standard deviation of X

- As $n$ increases, the random variable $\bar{x}$ of sample means, tends to be normally distributed

$\bar{x} \sim \Bigg(\micro_x, \frac{\sigma_X}{\sqrt{n}}\Bigg)$

- The normal distribution has the same mean as the original distribution and a variance that equals to the original variance divided by the sample size
- If $n$ samples are drawn, the distribution of the random variable $\bar{x}$, that consists of sample means, is called the sampling distribution of the mean
- The sampling distribution of the mean approaches a normal distributions, as the sample size increases

$z = \frac{{\bar{x} - \micro_x}}{(\frac{\sigma_X}{\sqrt{n}})}$

$\sigma_x = \frac{\sigma_X}{\sqrt{n}}$ => standard error of the mean

# The Central Limit Theorem for Sums

- If random samples are drawn, the random variable $\sum X$ consisting of the sums tends ot be normally distributed

$\sum X\sim N((n)(\micro_X), (\sqrt{n}(\sigma_X))$

- The normal distribution has a mean equal to the original mean multiplied by the sample size and a standard deviation equal to the original standard deviation multiplied by the square root of the sample size

$z = \frac{\sum x - (n)(\micro_X)}{(\sqrt{n})(\sigma_X)}$

$(n)(\micro_X)$ = mean of $\sum X$
$(\sqrt{n})(\sigma_X)$ = standard deviation of $\sum X$

# Using the Central Limit Theorem

- Application of CLT
	- Find the probability of the mean
	- Find the probability of a sum or total
	- Not used for an individual value
