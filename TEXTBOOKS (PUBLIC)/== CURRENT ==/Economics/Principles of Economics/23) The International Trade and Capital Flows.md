
- Balance of trade
	- Any gap between a nation's dollar value of its exports, or what its producers sell abroad, and a nation's dollar value of imports
- If exports exceed imports, the economy has a trade surplus
- If imports exceed exports, the economy has a trade deficit

- A unbalanced of trade can lead to financial crises and deep recessions
- Large trade deficits
	- Foreign investors become pessimistic about the economy and move their money to other countries
	- Real GDP decreases

## Measuring Trade Balances

- Merchandise trade balance
	- Tracking of solid or physical items that planes, trains, and trucks transported between countries
	- Way to measure the balance of trade
	- In most high-income economies, goods comprise less than half of the country's total production, while services make up the other halve
- Current account balance
	- Measure that includes other international flows of income and foreign aid

## Trade Balances and Flows of Financial Capital

### Understanding the Determinants of the Trade and Current Account Balance

- National saving and investment identity
	- Provides a useful way to understand the determinants of the trade and current account balance
- A country's national savings is the total of its domestic savings by household and companies as well as the government
- If a country is running a trade deficit, it means money from abroad is entering the country and the government considers it part of the supply of financial capital

**2 Main Sources of Supply for US Economy**
- Savings by individuals and firms (S)
- Inflow of financial capital from foreign investors (M - X)

**2 Main Source of Demand for US Economy**
- Private sector investment (I)
- Government borrowing (G)

### Short-Term Movements in the Business Cycle and the Trade Balance

- A recession tends to make the trade deficit smaller, or a trade surplus larger
- While a period of economic growth makes a trade deficit larger, or a trade surplus smaller
- During a recession the economy slows down and purchases fewer goods

## The Pros and Cons of Trade Deficits and Surpluses

- Flows of international trade are actually the same as flows of international financial capital
- Borrowing at certain times can also make sound economic sense
- A borrower nation can find itself in a bind if it does not invest the incoming funds abroad in a way that leads to increased productivity
- Extreme departure of foreign capital can cause banking systems and economies to fall
	- Lead to recession

## The Difference Between Level of Trade and the Trade Balance

- A high level of trade indicates that the nation exports a good portion of its production
- A country's level of trade represents its production of exports
	- Economy's globalization
- Factors of trade level
	- Size of economy
	- Geographic location
	- History of trade
