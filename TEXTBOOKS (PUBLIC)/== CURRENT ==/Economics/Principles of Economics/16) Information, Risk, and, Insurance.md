
- Asymmetric information affects markets for goods, labour, and financial capital
- When buyers have less information about the quality of the good than sellers do, sellers may be tempted to mislead buyers
- Alternatively, buyers are reluctant or unwilling to purchase the products

## The Problem of Imperfect Information and Asymmetric Information

- Asymmetric information
	- When two parties involved in an economic transaction have an unequal amount of information
- Imperfect information
	- Where either the buyer, the seller, or both, are less than 100% certain about the qualities of what they are buying and selling
	- Causes a decline in prices or quantities of product sold

### "Lemons" and Other Examples of Imperfect Information

### How Imperfect Information Can Affect Equilibrium Price and Quantity

- Imperfect information can discourage both buyers and sellers from participating in the market
- Thin market
	- Few buyers and sellers
	- Opposite of thick market

### When Price Mixes with Imperfect Information about Quality

- A buyer can assume an item is authentic if it costs more
- When buyers use the market price to draw inferences about the products' quality, then markets may have trouble reaching an equilibrium price and quantity
- These model counters the basic model of demand and supply
	- Although there is a natural limit
- Information can become more widely known

### Mechanisms to Reduce the Risk of Imperfect Information

- Money-back-guarantee
	- An agreement that functions as a promise of quality
- Warranty
	- Promises to fix or replace the goods, for a period of time
- Service contract
	- The buyers pays an extra amount and the seller agrees to fix anything that goes wrong for a set time period
- These are examples of explicit reassurances that sellers provide
- Unstated guarantees
	- Refunds
	- Exchanges
	- Store credit
- Occupational licenses
	- Establish quality in the labour market
	- Represent a barrier to entry to certain industries
- Federal Trade Commission (FTC)
	- Allows advertising to contain a certain amount of exaggeration about the general delight of using a product
- Cosigner
	- Another person or firm who legally pledge to replay some or all of the money if the original borrower does not do so
- Collateral
	- Property or equipment that the bank would have the right to seize and sell if borrower does not replay the lean

### Risk Groups and Actuarial Fairness

- Risk group
	- Defined as a group that shares roughly the same risks of an adverse event occurring
- Charge lower premiums with those with lower risks, and higher to those with higher risks

### The Moral Hazard Problem

- Moral hazard
	- The case when people engage in riskier behaviour with insurance than without
- Monitor behaviour
- Offer lower rate on property insurance for businesses that have use additional services
- Deductibles
	- An amount that the insurance policyholder must pay out of their own packet before the insurance coverage starts paying
- Copayment
	- Policyholder must pay a small amount
- Coinsurance
	- The insurance company covers a certain percentage of the cost
- Fee-for-service
	- Services providers are paid for the service they provide, and more for additional services
	- Health services
- Structure of healthcare provision has shifted to health maintenance organization (HMOs)
	- Provides healthcare that received a fixed amount per person enrolled in the plan, regardless of service provided

### The Adverse Selection Problem

- Adverse selection
	- The problem in which insurance buyers have more information about whether they are high risk of low risk than the insurance company does

### Government Regulation of Insurance

- The US insurance industry is primarily regulated at the state level
- National Association of Insurance Commissioners
- Keep price of insurance low and ensure that everyone has insurance
- The average amount individuals receive cannot exceed the average amount paid in premiums
- With low premiums, insurance companies avoid high-risk parties
- Government regulators cannot force companies to change low prices and provide high levels of insurance coverage
- Taxpayers and insurance buyers can help make up the difference in insurance groups

### The Patient Protection and Affordable Care Act

- Patient Protection and Affordable Care Act (PPACA)
	- Get US closer to universal health coverage
	- Individual mandate
	- Each state is required to have health insurance exchanges
	- Employer mandate
- Affordable Card Act (ACA)
	- Increasing the Medicare Tax by 0.9& and adding a 3.8% tax on unearned income for high income taxpayers
	- Annual fee on health insurance providers
	- Imposing other taxes on manufacturers and importers of certain medical devices
